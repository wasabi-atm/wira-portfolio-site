Project Background

We’ve all been in traffic that just makes us wants to pull our hair out due to stress. Carma - Driving Mindfulness is an app that can help users stressed by traffic or those who wants to be mindful while driving.

Carma will deliver calming sounds alongside audio prompts for mindfulness driving without distracting the users with the phone’s screen & notifications.

The Key Problem

It’s quite difficult to determine the most effective way to calm down the user while they’re still supposed to be focused on the road.

There was a few ideas I thought that can solve the problem with their own questions I ask myself:

  1. Guided mindfulness only when vehicle stops.
    1. What if the traffic was clear?
    2. What if the traffic jam was so heavy that the mindfulness prompts gets repetitive?
    3. What if traffic was like a jammed faucet? How is the trigger?
  2. Breathing exercises for before/after the drive.
    1. What if user don’t feel like opening the app after the drive?
    2. What if the user forgot to open the app before the drive?
    3. What if the breathing exercise be?
    4. Mindfulness before/after drive might not be useful if user is in a hurry, how should we tackle this?
  3. Guided mindfulness during the drive (not dividing focus).
    1. How should the mindfulness be so it’s not distracting during the drive?
    2. What should be on the screen during driving so it doesn’t distract her?

In this case…

I decided to go with solution 3, guided mindfulness during the drive as this proposal doesn’t have as much issue when tackling with the unpredictability of traffic.

User Personas

Persona 1: The Daily Commuter

Driving Frequency: Twice daily (home ↔ office)

Goals:

  • Reduce stress during traffic-heavy commutes
  • Feel more present and calm before meetings
  • Avoid phone distractions

Frustrations:

  • Heavy traffic on the way home increases irritability
  • Gets anxious before big work presentations
  • Has tried meditation apps but finds them hard to use while driving

Needs from Carma:

  • A hands-free experience
  • Soothing audio cues that don’t require attention to the screen
  • Mindfulness practices that align with her driving schedule

Persona 2: The Ride-Share Driver

Driving Frequency: 6–8 hours daily

Goals:

  • Stay mentally fresh throughout long driving shifts
  • Deal with occasional rude passengers and unpredictable traffic
  • Have short, effective mental resets between rides

Frustrations:

  • Feels mentally drained by the end of the day
  • Doesn’t want to fiddle with apps while working
  • Music helps but sometimes isn’t enough to keep him calm

Needs from Carma:

  • Simple audio triggers between rides or while waiting for next passenger
  • Background calming soundscapes
  • Optional brief breathing or mindfulness sessions during downtimes

Persona 3: The Mindful Parent

Driving Frequency: Daily school runs + errands

Goals:

  • Stay patient and calm during school traffic jams
  • Use driving time as a personal mental break
  • Keep her kids calm in the back seat too

Frustrations:

  • Stressful drop-offs with noisy traffic
  • Lack of quiet, mindful time during the day
  • Kids sometimes distract her while driving

Needs from Carma:

  • Gentle guided mindfulness that’s family-friendly
  • Passive audio content that doesn’t interfere with conversations
  • Option to use before and after driving when parked

Problem Statement

“Drivers experiencing stress during traffic often find it difficult to stay calm and present without becoming distracted by their phones. They need a hands-free, passive mindfulness solution that helps reduce anxiety and improve focus without interfering with their driving.”

User Journey Map

To better understand how Carma could fit into a commuter’s daily life, I mapped out the user journey of Persona 1: The Daily Commuter. This visual outlines the emotional highs and lows across different stages of their drive—from preparing to leave home, to navigating traffic, to arriving at work. It highlights key pain points such as pre-drive anxiety, stress during stop-and-go traffic, and mental clutter before entering the office.

By identifying these emotional touchpoints, I was able to design moments where Carma could provide the most value—through calming soundscapes, subtle mindfulness prompts, and passive support that doesn’t require screen interaction. This journey map helped anchor design decisions around real user needs and revealed when and how mindfulness support should be delivered during a typical commute.

User Interview

A friend that’s always feel stressed during traffic jam resulting in them having a bad mood for the rest of the day

To better understand the emotional and practical challenges of commuting, I conducted a one-on-one in-depth interview with a daily car commuter who regularly experiences traffic in Denpasar. The goal was to uncover how traffic jams impact her mood, routines, and mental well-being—and what opportunities might exist for a mindfulness-based intervention.

Key Insights:

  • Routine Stress: The interviewee finds parts of her morning routine—like opening the gate and dealing with disorganized traffic—mentally taxing before she even starts driving.
  • Emotional Exhaustion: She often feels overwhelmed, mad, or shocked by how other drivers behave. This leads to emotional fatigue that carries into her day.
  • Coping Mechanisms: Her main strategy to release stress is swearing or venting, which she says helps her feel better temporarily.
  • Minimal Relief from Techniques: She has tried breathing exercises, but found they help only slightly.
  • Need for Acceptance & Calm: She acknowledges the need to “let go” and accept what she can’t control in traffic, suggesting openness to mindfulness-based approaches.
  • Infrastructure & Environment Matter: Better infrastructure in Semarang compared to Bali makes her feel more secure, but other drivers’ behavior and hot, unpredictable weather still impact her mood.

Opportunities Identified:

  • A need for emotional regulation tools that feel accessible and effortless during stressful moments.
  • An opportunity to shift traffic time into a reflective or calming space—especially if audio-based and hands-free.
  • A chance to reduce emotional burnout by offering micro-mindfulness breaks or check-ins before or after driving.

This qualitative data helped shape both the app’s core value proposition (a calmer commute) and the emphasis on audio-first, low-interaction design.

Hi-Fi Prototype

After validating key ideas through sketches and early lo-fi wireframes, I designed the high-fidelity prototype in Figma to closely simulate the final user experience. My main priority was to ensure that the UI would support mindfulness during driving without causing distractions or requiring screen interaction.

Design Priorities:

  • Minimalist Visuals: The interface features large touch targets and high-contrast elements with design hierarchy in mind.
  • Single-Tap Interactions: Users can start or stop a mindfulness session with a single, prominent button—ideal for quick, safe actions while stationary.
  • Audio-First Experience: The prototype emphasized sound as the primary medium—using subtle dark gradients so it doesn’t demand attention while being used while driving.
  • No Navigation Overload: I intentionally removed unnecessary menus or deep navigation layers to avoid complex interactions.

What’s Included in the Hi-Fi Prototype:

  • A calming home screen with a central play button and gentle visual feedback of button being pressed down & breathing animated opacity.
  • An onboarding flow that briefly introduces the app’s goals and permissions (e.g., motion access).
  • A mood check-in screen shown optionally after a session ends, letting users reflect briefly before moving on.
  • A minimal settings screen to control audio preferences and toggle optional features like breathing reminders.

Result:

The hi-fi prototype helped me gather meaningful feedback in user testing and served as a clear visual reference when building the actual iOS version in Xcode.

Xcode

Overview:

I developed a functional iOS prototype using Xcode and Swift. This allowed me to test the app’s real-world behavior, especially around passive audio playback and minimal screen interaction.

Key Features Implemented:

  • Background Audio Playback: Used AVAudioPlayer to play calming soundscapes even when the app is in the background or the screen is off.
  • Minimal UI for Safety: Built a distraction-free interface using SwiftUI, showing only essential controls like a large Play/Pause button.
  • Hands-Free Interaction: Implemented voice-over support and minimized touch interactions to align with driving safety guidelines.
  • State Management: Used @State and ObservableObject in SwiftUI to manage mindfulness session states and audio control.
  • Onboarding System: Used @AppStorage with hasSeenOnboarding status for basic logic of app.
  • Trigger System (Planned): Began experimentation with CoreMotion to eventually allow mindfulness prompts to adjust based on traffic speed patterns (e.g., stop-and-go detection).

Development Challenges:

  • Handling background audio without draining battery or interfering with other audio sources (like car radios or calls).
  • Balancing UX simplicity with necessary control—too few options vs. too many.
